Installing and using DirectShot

Back

DirectShot is a technology that permit to acquire a picture from a digital camera in a very fast way. This technology can only be used with Kodak™ Digital Cameras.

eMedia supports the TWain specification for image acquisition. But TWain needs user actions, such as selecting the picture, asking for data transmission, etc. For a picture taken from a digital camera, the user must first take the picture, then ask eMedia to retrieve it from the camera and finally delete the picture from the camera.

DirectShot uses a Kodak™ built-in technology that permit eMedia to "talk" directly to the digital camera, telling it to initialize, take the picture, transfer it back and delete it from the camera memory. You need one of the following Kodak™ digital cameras with drivers installed on the computer:

  • DC 200
  • DC 200 Plus
  • DC 210
  • DC 210 Plus
  • DC 215
  • DC 220
  • DC 240
  • DC 260
  • DC 265
  • DC 280
  • DC 3400
  • DC 5000

To use DirectShot

  • Connect the digital camera to the computer, using the Serial or USB cable provided. Place the camera in "connect" mode and power it on.
  • In eMedia, once the connection mode configured from the Edit menu, open your .emc design file in exploitation mode.
  • On the photo object, click on the "Aquire" button.
  • A window will open and will tell you what the software is doing. Once the camera ready, simply click on the "Take the picture" button to take the picture, transfer it back to the photo object and delete it from the memory card.

The DirectShot technology is fully asynchronous. You can continue to work with your card while the photo is taken and transfered.

The DirectShot technology is simple to use. As user, you will have only to click on the "Acquire" button to prepare the photo, then to click on the "Take the picture" button.

To install DirectShot

1.  Insert the eMedia installation CD.
2.  With Windows Explorer, browse the CD to the \eMedia\DirectShot folder.
3.  Double-click on the DCGenAX.exe file.
4.  Follow the instructions.
5. Copy the DirectShot.exe file into the eMedia installation folder on your hard disk.
6. Double-click on the DirectShot.exe file copied on your hard disk. The about box will be displayed. Click "OK" to close it.
7. DirectShot is now installed and can be used in eMedia.

To uninstall DirectShot

1.  With the Windows Explorer, display the installation folder on the hard disk.
2.  Open the "Start" menu, select "Run".
3.  In the "Run" dialog-box, clear the text-box contents.
4.  Drag the DirectShot.exe file from the Windows Explorer to the text-box in the "Run" dialog-box. The full filename will be displayed.
5. Add to the text-box, at the end: " /UNREGSERVER" (don't write the quotes, but begin with a space character).
6. Click the "OK" button.
7. Open the "Start" menu, select "Settings", "Control Panel".
8. Double-click on the "Add/Remove Programs" icon.
9. Click on the "Kodak DCGen ActiveX, then on the "Remove" button.
10. DirectShot is now uninstalled. eMedia still works, but the DirectShot menus are now disabled.
